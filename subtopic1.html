URLThe Online Behaviour Taxonomy: A conceptual framework to understand behaviour in computer-mediated communication
Author(s)Linda K. Kaye, Anastasia Rousaki, Laura C. Joyner, Laura A.F. Barrett, Lisa J. Orchard
Abstract: This peer reviewed journal article written by Linda K. Kaye, Anastasia Rousaki, Laura C. Joyner, Laura A.F. Barrett, and Lisa J. Orchard describes the relationship between human behaviors and communication in computer-mediated spaces like social media and other online platforms. The article focuses on computer-mediated communication or CMC which is a theory that uses the cyberpsychological perspective to understand online behaviors and interactions. The article goes on to break down an original taxonomy that the authors created to define three types of behaviors that consisted of the Online-exclusive behaviors, Online-mediated behavior, and Online-recorded behavior. The three behavior types categorize interactions individuals have with the digital world that can later be used to compare, research, and further analyze.

URLEncourage positive online behaviour
Author(s)ReachOut Content Team
Abstract: This webpage was written by the ReachOut Content Team. ReachOut is an organization that's based in Australia. ReachOut created a platform that is a safe place where young people can connect with others, get one on one peer support, learn and deepen their knowledge on life experiences, and more. The small webpage article was written on encouraging positive digital behaviors. The authors detail different steps young individuals can take to show positive digital behaviors and provide examples. They focus on topics like protecting privacy, connecting on social media safely and respectfully, and practicing accountability. ReachOut provides a helpful and easy to follow guide for young teenagers.
